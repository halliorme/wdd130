Rivers

The Main Fork of the Salmon River in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return holds a rich history and stunning natural beauty. The river, nicknamed the River of No Return, owes its moniker to the allure of its rugged beauty, captivating visitors who find it hard to leave. Besides the breathtaking landscape, the area teems with wildlife—elk, deer, bear, and bighorn sheep often grace the hillsides. Towering ponderosa pines line the river corridor, offering shade at the sandy campsites, creating an ideal shelter from the elements. The rapids, ranging from Class I to IV, punctuate the experience along one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States. Its flow varies across seasons, offering a thrilling ride in early summer with higher, faster, and colder waters, while late summer brings slower, warmer waters—perfect for families with children who enjoy swimming.

Desolation and Gray Canyons in central Utah, carved by the winding Green River, present a landscape of stark beauty. The canyons, amidst some of the most remote land, boast high grey stone cliffs, desert scenery, and fantastic rapids that have made this stretch renowned. Spanning 84 miles from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid outside Green River Utah, this trip offers exceptional scenery, evidence of human history, and thrilling whitewater opportunities. Desolation Canyon, a Class II/III rating, typically spans a six-day journey, earning recognition as a National Landmark for its unique history and geological formations. This designation celebrates the rich narrative woven by the canyon's past and the awe-inspiring stories it tells.
